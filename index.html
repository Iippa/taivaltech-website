The problem

Hardware startups rarely fail for lack of ideas. They stall because senior engineering time is scarce and expensive at exactly the wrong moment — during a critical design review, a supplier negotiation, or the first production run.

Large consultancies bring overhead, layers, and junior engineers on the actual work. Freelancers are available, but rarely carry the depth that complex electromechanical systems require.

The gap is real: access to experienced, independent hardware judgment at the pace a startup actually operates.
